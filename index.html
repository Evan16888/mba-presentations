Executive Summary

Client

Apple Inc., the world's most valuable technology company, with a market capitalisation exceeding $3 trillion and annual revenue of $383 billion in FY2024 (Apple, 2024).

Problem

iPhone market share in China declined from 20.3% to 15.6% (Q4 2023–Q4 2025), losing its position to Huawei and domestic competitors (IDC, 2025; Counterpoint, 2025).

Root Causes

Huawei's resurgence with Kirin-powered devices, growing domestic brand preference, perceived innovation gap (no foldable, delayed AI), and macroeconomic headwinds (Gurman, 2025).

Proposed Solutions

Accelerate localised Apple Intelligence features, introduce competitive mid-tier pricing, deepen ecosystem integration with Chinese platforms (Ives, 2025).

Analysis

Selected Leaf: Enhanced Trade-in Programmes

Research Question: Does increasing trade-in value by 20% drive measurable conversion uplift in China?

Analysis Approach

A comparative analysis using secondary data from Counterpoint Research (2025) and Apple's quarterly revenue segmentation for Greater China. Historical trade-in participation rates (2022–2025) were correlated with quarter-on-quarter iPhone unit sales using regression modelling.

Findings

Markets with aggressive trade-in promotions (Japan, India) showed a 12–18% increase in upgrade rates within two quarters (Counterpoint, 2025). During 2024 Singles' Day, where trade-in subsidies were temporarily increased, iPhone sales surged by 21% week-on-week on JD.com (SCMP, 2024). Huawei's trade-in programme contributed to 28% of its Mate 60 sales (Canalys, 2025).

💡 Key Insight

A permanent 20% increase in trade-in values could recover approximately 2–3 percentage points of market share within 12 months — contributing ~40% of the target recovery from 15.6% to 19%. This is effective because Apple's existing user loyalty remains high at 82% (CIRP, 2025), converting retention into upgrade revenue.

One-Day Answer & References

Situation

Context

Apple's iPhone market share in China declined from 20.3% to 15.6% (2023–2025), driven by Huawei's resurgence, domestic brand preference, and a perceived lag in AI innovation (IDC, 2025).

Complication

Risk

China ($67B FY2024) is Apple's 3rd-largest market. If current trends continue, Apple risks losing 3–5% more share by 2028 — equating to $12–15B in annual revenue erosion (Morgan Stanley, 2025).

Resolution

Action

A three-pronged strategy: (1) Baidu LLM partnership for localised AI, (2) permanent enhanced trade-in programmes (+15% conversion), (3) Douyin/Xiaohongshu campaigns — collectively recovering 3.4+ pp within 24 months (Ives, 2025).
